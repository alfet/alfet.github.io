Special Projects

I've written a lot of stories, but there are a few special projects I've worked on that I'd like to highlight and show off.

  • When & Where: Syracuse University Crime data map
  • Date: April 2015
  • Description: After collecting data from the university's Department of Public Safety's crime logs, I created a database of the crimes relevant to students on campus. Using this data, I created the maps using CartoDB and the interactive charts with HighCharts and customized them using open source codes. The Daily Orange published three stories on this data, illustrating trends through times and locations.

  • "A Tale of Two Cities": New York City homicide data map
  • Date: January 2014
  • Description: I compiled the NYPD's data on reported homicides in 2013, with as much information as I could find. This included where and how the murders happened, the victim's demographic and the investigation status. I interviewed families whose relatives' murders were still unsolved because of their neighborhood. I worked on this project with several other reporters and editors.

  • "Barred From Home": New York City Nuisance Abatement Laws
  • Date: February 2016
  • Description: I contributed to the New York Daily News' Pulitzer Prize winning project on Nuisance Abatement laws. I was assigned to search through Bronx Court records from 2005 to 2015 showcasing situations where the NYPD used an obscure law to kick families out of homes for crimes they were never found guilty of.
